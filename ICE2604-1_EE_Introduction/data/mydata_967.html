In mathematics, Stirling's approximation (or Stirling's formula) is an approximation for factorials. It is a good approximation, leading to accurate results even for small values of n {\displaystyle n} . It is named after James Stirling, though a related but less precise result was first stated by Abraham de Moivre.[1][2][3]
